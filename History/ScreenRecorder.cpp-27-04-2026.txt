History — ScreenRecorder.cpp
=============================

[27/04/2026]
  Fixed audio/video synchronisation drift.

  Root cause: video timestamps were derived from frameIndex × framePeriod
  (a virtual frame-counter clock), while audio timestamps were derived by
  accumulating WASAPI packet durations (an independent clock). The two clocks
  drift apart indefinitely — increasingly bad sync the longer the recording.

  Changes made:

  1. CaptureFrame() — video timestamp now computed from QPC elapsed since
     recording start (same origin clock as audio).
       Before: ts = m_videoFrameIndex.load() * m_framePeriod
       After:  ts = (now.QuadPart - m_qpcRecordingStart.QuadPart) * 10'000'000LL / m_qpcFreq.QuadPart

  2. AudioCaptureThread() — removed video-relative gap-fill block. GetBuffer()
     now captures qpcPos (UINT64). Each packet's timestamp is derived from
     qpcPos relative to m_qpcRecordingStart (same clock as video). Silence is
     inserted only for real gaps detected by comparing consecutive QPC values.

  3. MicCaptureThread() — identical fix to AudioCaptureThread() applied to the
     separate mic audio stream (m_micAudioTimestamp / m_pMicCaptureClient).

[27/04/2026 — second edit]
  Reverted broken qpcPos approach; correct fix applied.

  Root cause of broken first fix: WASAPI loopback streams can return qpcPos=0
  from GetBuffer() when the device does not support position tracking (documented
  MSDN behaviour). This stamped every audio packet at t=0, making audio play
  entirely at the start of the recording — completely out of sync.

  To revert to working state, restore changes 2 & 3 above back to accumulated
  sample durations and remove qpcPos. Change 1 (QPC video timestamps) is CORRECT
  and must be kept.

  Changes made in this edit:

  1. CaptureFrame() — QPC video timestamp RETAINED (correct from first edit).

  2. AudioCaptureThread() — reverted GetBuffer() to (nullptr, nullptr); no qpcPos.
     Audio timestamps use accumulated sample durations from t=0 (reliable, exact).
     Gap detection now uses AUDCLNT_BUFFERFLAGS_DATA_DISCONTINUITY: when WASAPI
     signals a real gap, audio clock is resynced to QPC wall clock and silence is
     inserted for the missing duration. Removed video-frame-counter gap-fill.

  3. MicCaptureThread() — same revert and discontinuity handling as above.
