17-05-2026 - cmake-build.bat

CHANGE: Added :killcompiler subroutine and call sites to release file handles
        left by any previously cancelled or failed build.

- New :killcompiler subroutine runs:
    taskkill /F /IM cl.exe       >nul 2>&1
    taskkill /F /IM mspdbsrv.exe >nul 2>&1
  Errors suppressed — harmless if no processes are running.

- Called at two points:
  1. Before cmake runs (primary guarantee — handles previous cancelled builds,
     since Ctrl+C in batch cannot be trapped reliably).
  2. At :error (cleanup for the build that just failed or was cancelled).

WHY: A cancelled MSVC build leaves cl.exe processes alive with open handles on
every header they were parsing. Those handles block subsequent file writes until
the processes are killed. Releasing handles at the start of the next build ensures
a clean state before new cl.exe instances are spawned.
